Red Wine Braised Beef

Ingredients

  • 3 to 4 lbs beef chuck roast or short ribs
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and freshly grounded black pepper, to taste
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 medium carrrots, peeled and chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chooped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups red wine
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons fresh thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped

Procedure

Recipe for Red Wine Braised Beef:
Prepare the Beef: Preheat your oven to 325°F (163°C). Pat the beef chuck roast dry with paper towels. Season generously with salt and pepper on all sides.
Sear the Beef: Heat olive oil in a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, sear the beef on all sides until browned. This caramelizes the meat and builds flavor. Remove the beef from the pot and set it aside.
Cook the Vegetables: In the same pot, add the chopped onion, carrots, and celery. Sauté for 5-7 minutes until softened, stirring occasionally. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
Deglaze the Pot: Add the tomato paste to the pot and cook for about 2 minutes to caramelize slightly. Pour in the red wine to deglaze the pot, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan with a wooden spoon. Bring the wine to a simmer and let it reduce by about half.
Braise the Beef: Add the beef back into the pot along with any juices that have accumulated on the plate. Pour in the beef broth, ensuring the beef is mostly covered with liquid. Add the thyme, bay leaves, and rosemary. Bring the mixture to a simmer.
Braise in the Oven: Cover the pot with a lid or foil, and transfer it to the preheated oven. Braise the beef for 2.5 to 3 hours, or until the meat is fork-tender and falls apart easily.
Finish the Sauce: Remove the pot from the oven. Take the beef out of the sauce and let it rest for 10 minutes before shredding or slicing. If the sauce is too thin, place the pot on the stovetop over medium heat and reduce it to your desired consistency. You can also stir in 1 tablespoon of butter for a richer sauce.
Serve: Slice or shred the braised beef, and spoon the sauce and vegetables over it.
